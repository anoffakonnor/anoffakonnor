Indicators: B7.3.3.1.1-4

B7.3.3.1.1: Determining shapes in real life that have reflectional (or fold) symmetries.

A line of symmetry is any line that divides objects or shapes in a plane into two equal parts. These two new objects or shapes will have the same shape and size, that is, they will look similar to each other. Hence, a line of symmetry is normally refered to as a mirror line.

For instance, when you look into a mirror, you see your image. So in this case the mirror is acting as a line between you (the object) and the other person you see inside of the mirror (the image).

Learn more

B7.3.3.1.2: Plot points and shapes (i.e. plane figures) on a coordinate plane and draw their images under reflection in given lines

In this lesson, we will learn how to plot coordinate points (ordered pairs) in the coordinate plane, other times called the cartesian plane. We will also learn how to use the x-axis and the y-axis as our mirror lines to find the reflection of the points we learned to plot on the cartesian plane.

In the cartesian plane, the x-axis is also called the line y = 0. This is because the x-axis cuts, or passes through the y-axis at the point where the y-axis is 0.

Similarly, the y-axis is also called the line x = 0 because it cuts the x-axis at the point where the x-axis is 0.

Remember, the image of a point has the same distance as its object, from the mirror line.

Learn more

B7.3.3.1.3: Plot points and shapes (i.e. plane figures) on a coordinate plane and draw their images under translation by a given vector.

Translation is the movement of a point or a shape within the cartesian plane. This movement is normally done in a straight line, and in some direction. Hence, we normally translate points by vectors in the cartesian plane.

A vector is any quantity that has a magnitude and a direction.

We will learn how to draw shapes and points in the cartesian plane and use a vector to translate it, that is, move the point or shape drawn to a different point within the cartesian plane, using a given vector.

Learn more

B7.3.3.1.4: Verify the concept of congruent and similar shapes in coordinate plane using properties of both the object(s) and image(s); and in real life situations (carpet designs, fabric pattern)

Two shapes are said to be congruent when the magnitude of their sides are equal

In this lesson, we will consider various shapes, determine the magnitude of their sides and decipher whether they are congruent shapes or not.
